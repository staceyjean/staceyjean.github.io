Weekly Report

Market Update:

  1. Contract Market:The contract price negotiation for April has already concluded. The bargaining momentum is very weak in the first week of May. Since the majority of inventory is still in the hands of suppliers and has not yet been transferred to buyers, there is still a downward pressure on prices, which is why trading volume remains low.
  2. Spot Market:Due to the majority of Asian holidays falling in the first week of May, trading volume is low, but there was also no significant price cuts. The price of the DDR4 1G*8 2666 Mbps mainstream chip dropped by 1.4% ($4.28-$4.22 USD) this week. Since May is the end of the quarter for Micron, the forecast is downward pressure on spot prices will gradually increase.
  3. Overview of Market Dynamics:In terms of smartphones, the shipment of high-density solutions is doing slightly better than other products (Chinese brands are launching flagship phones). Therefore, the decline in mobile DRAM prices this week is not significant. As for servers, the Chinese brands BAT show weak stock-up momentum, and North American buyers have not shown much demand ever since the pull-in peak in March.

TrendForce’ s View：

  • Around the end of April, the smartphone brand Apple made long-term agreements (LTA) with DRAM manufacturers to secure DRAM supply for the second half of the year. According to TrendForce, the main target of the LTA is SK Hynix, and the focus is mostly on DRAM memory rather than NAND Flash.
